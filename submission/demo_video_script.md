## 0:00 – 0:25 | The Problem

\[Show: title slide or app landing page]

> "Antimicrobial resistance — AMR — is one of the top threats to global health, linked to over a million deaths a year. The core issue: when a patient arrives with a suspected infection, doctors must pick an antibiotic *before* lab culture results come back, often 48 to 72 hours later. Pick wrong, and treatment fails, the infection worsens, and resistant bacteria spread even faster."

***

## 0:25 – 0:45 | User Target

\[Show: patient form, sidebar model selector]

> "Our target users are frontline clinicians — ER physicians, hospitalists, and ICU teams — who make this empirical prescribing decision daily. Their pain point: existing tools are static, hospital-wide antibiograms that don't account for *this specific patient's* labs, comorbidities, or prior antibiotic exposure. Current solutions are too generic to be trusted at the bedside."

***

## 0:45 – 1:10 | The Solution

\[Show: brief scroll through README architecture diagram or app overview]

> "Our solution predicts *patient-specific* antibiotic resistance probabilities and turns them into an explainable, actionable recommendation in seconds. It combines a calibrated machine learning ensemble — LightGBM, XGBoost, CatBoost, and HistGradientBoosting, validated in our peer-reviewed research published in *Discover AI*, Springer Nature — with SHAP explainability and an AI reasoning agent powered by LLaMA 3.3."

***

## 1:10 – 1:35 | Architecture & Responsible AI

\[Show: architecture diagram from README, or quickly point to the three pipeline stages in the app]

> "The pipeline has three stages: the ML model scores every candidate antibiotic; SHAP explains *which* patient features drove that score; and our clinical decision agent ranks the options and applies a confidence gate based on paper-validated thresholds. For responsible AI, the system never overrides clinical judgement — every recommendation is explainable, traceable to real patient data, and if no antibiotic is confidently safe, the system refuses to guess and instead flags the case for specialist review."

***
